My crusade as a designer began in 2006 creating infographics for a newspaper. Since then, Iâ€™ve consistently challenged myself to produce stronger and more memorable visuals and refine my creative process through the diversity each project presents.


Regarding style, I'm a devout acolyte in the "less is more" design philosophy and believe that like fractions, if it can be reduced or simplified then it should. One less moving piece on a machine means one less piece to fail and every piece required serves a purpose.

Creating a company's image from scratch requires a lot of checked boxes as designs need to be versatile for use thoughout all aspects of marketing; website banner, social media badge, letter template, business cards, coffee mug, t-shirt, sticker, etc. Any lack of consistency and it fails to establish brand identity.

A few of the questions I ask to determine if a logo design is effective are:

Is it still effective without color?

Can it scale and maintain clarity?

Does it align with the company's image?

Does it align with the company's audience?

Is there anything I can subtract/simplify?
